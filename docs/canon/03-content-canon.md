# 03 · Content Canon — Question Bank v3
*The product's content, verbatim. Edits to any question happen here and in code in the same change. Format: `id [answer-type] text` — hints omitted for budget; hints live in code and follow law 3.*

## Stage 1 · The Problem — *The Call to Adventure* · Swirling Nebula
Rule: the Vault is active — solution words (app, platform, feature, AI, build, tool, SaaS…) trigger a gentle nudge → capture to Vault, return to the problem.
- s1-th [story] Tell the last time you watched someone hit this problem. Who were they — name them — where were they, and what did they do next?
- s1-l1 [names] Who exactly has this problem? Three real people or organizations. No personas, no 'busy professionals.'
- s1-l2 [fivewhys] Why is that a problem for them? *(chained ×5 to a visible root)*
- s1-l3 [number] What does one occurrence cost — in minutes, dollars, or dignity? How often does it happen?
- s1-l4 [list] What do they do about it today? The workaround is your first competitor.
- s1-l5 [story] Why you? What have you lived, seen, or built that makes this problem yours to carry?
- s1-fp [quickadd] Strip it bare. List only what you know from direct observation — not reports, not belief. Everything else goes to the Assumption Registry. *(inline "This only works if ___" → guardian)*
- s1-fx [falsify] If this problem weren't worth solving, what would the world look like? Do you see any of that?
Milestones: story with a named person · three real people named · Five-Whys root reached.

## Stage 2 · Research — *Meeting the Mentor* · The Raven
Field rules banner: *Talk about their life, not your idea. Past specifics, not future hypotheticals. Compliments are not data.*
- s2-th [verbatim] Ask five people living this problem about the last time it happened — not whether they would use your idea. Paste what they said, word for word. *(+ "Log a quote as E2" shortcut)*
- s2-l1 What's their current 'good enough' — the spreadsheet, the cousin, the doing-nothing? What does keeping it cost them?
- s2-l2 What did you hear that you didn't want to hear? Log that first.
- s2-l3 [names] Who profits from this problem existing? Who loses if it is solved?
- s2-l4 Name the one external shift — a rule, a platform policy, a price, a technology — that could make this venture pointless or impossible within two years. How likely is it?
- s2-l5 Who has walked this exact terrain? What's the one question you'd ask them — and have you sent it? *(hint: experience over expertise — a veteran of the current workaround counts)*
**The Fellowship — People Over Idea** *(PIE: People 5× / Idea 2× / Enchantment 1.5×)*
- s2-p1 [story] When did you last change your mind because someone pushed back? What did it cost you?
- s2-p2 [story] What's the disagreement you and your cofounder keep not having? Have it — then write what each of you actually said. (Solo? Argue with the voice that disagrees, and transcribe.)
- s2-p3 [names] Who — besides you — is illogically enchanted by this? Name them. If no one, log that in the ledger too.
- s2-p4 [list] What are you bringing that helps everyone else succeed — before anyone helps you?
- s2-fx [falsify] What pattern across your five conversations would tell you this problem is real but not urgent? Do you see it?
Milestones: five E2+ conversations · one E3/E4 entry · riskiest guardian with kill criterion.

**⛩ Act I Gate — The First Threshold** (after Stage 2): Stage-1 threshold answered · ≥5 E2+ entries · ≥1 E3/E4 · a guardian with a written kill criterion. Warn, never block; override = written reason, logged, exported.

## Stage 3 · Prototyping — *The Approach* · The Phoenix *(the Vault unseals)*
- s3-th Write your customer's sentence in their words from your ledger: 'When I [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome].'
- s3-l1 [vault] Open the Vault. Which captured idea attacks the root cause from your Five Whys — not a symptom of it?
- s3-l2 [ifthen] State the logic before you build: IF ___ / THEN when [named segment] meets [prototype], we will observe [behavior] / WITHIN [days]. *(+ "Register the IF as a guardian")*
- s3-l3 What's the smallest thing you can put in front of a real customer in 7 days that lets them answer with behavior — a sketch, a fake door, a concierge run you do by hand?
- s3-l4 [list] Which features are for the customer, and which are for your ego? Write the second list — that's what goes into the flames.
- s3-l5 Am I building to learn, or building to be admired?
- s3-joy **[The Spark of Joy]** Beyond killing the pain — what one moment could make them smile and tell a friend? Name the moment. Design it on purpose.
Milestones: JTBD in their words · IF-THEN stated before building · 7-day artifact chosen.

## Stage 4 · Testing — *Crossing the Threshold* · The Labyrinth
- s4-th [seal] Before anything runs: write the result that makes you stop or pivot. Seal it. This is Ariadne's Thread. *(timestamped, locked; two-step confirm)*
- s4-l1 What behavior are you measuring — not opinions you are collecting? Clicks, sign-ups, prepayments, returns, time-on-task.
- s4-l2 What does a costly yes look like — money, a deposit, a calendar hold, an intro to their boss? Compliments are not currency.
- s4-l3 [story] Where did testers get lost — and what did they do in the ten seconds before quitting?
- s4-l4 [falsify] If the test 'succeeds', what's the strongest boring explanation — novelty, politeness, the wrong crowd? How do you rule it out?
- s4-l5 What's the smallest step you can take today?
Milestones: thread sealed before testing · behavior metric defined · test run with real users.

## Stage 5 · Feedback — *Tests, Allies & Enemies* · The Mirror
- s5-th [verdict] Open the seal. Did Ariadne's Thread trigger — yes or no? Answer before you interpret anything else. *(shows the sealed text; verdict invites the Council)*
- s5-l1 What uncomfortable truths is the mirror showing me? *(v2 verbatim)*
- s5-l2 Am I listening to the market, or protecting my own ego? *(v2 verbatim)*
- s5-l3 What is the gap between my intention and their perception? *(v2 verbatim)*
- s5-l4 Take the most inconvenient entry in your ledger and argue its case like you are its lawyer. What if it is right?
- s5-l5 [registry] Which Stage-1 belief is now dead? Hold the funeral: mark it invalidated in the Registry — and take the XP.
- s5-dec [decision] Pivot, or persevere? Cite the evidence that decides it. *(locked until ≥1 citation)*
Milestones: verdict recorded · one funeral held · decision cited to evidence.

**⛩ Act II Gate — The Mirror's Verdict** (after Stage 5): verdict recorded · pivot/persevere decided with ≥1 citation.

## Stage 6 · Refinement — *The Ordeal* · The Sculptor
- s6-th What do users actually do with it, versus what you built it for? Cut everything serving only the second.
- s6-l1 [number] What one action must a new user complete to feel the value? Count the steps, seconds, and decisions standing in the way.
- s6-l2 If you fix one thing this week, what does the evidence — not your taste — say it is?
**The Unseen — Ethical Impact**
- s6-u1 [names] Who is affected but not in the room? Name them. What would they say if they read your plan?
- s6-u2 How would a bad actor use this exactly as designed? What is the cheapest guardrail, built now while it is cheap?
- s6-u3 What behavior does your revenue model reward at scale? Are you at peace with what it optimizes?
- s6-u4 Whose data do you touch — and what is the least of it you can hold?
Milestones: cuts from observed use · time-to-value counted · one guardrail named.

## Stage 7 · Implementation — *The Road Back* · The Bridge
- s7-th [number] Walk one customer across the bridge — one month, real figures. What do they pay, what does serving them cost, what remains? Any number you do not know is an assumption: register it. *(+ quick-add guardian)*
- s7-l1 Has anyone paid, pre-paid, or given up something costly — time, data, a deposit, an introduction? What is the closest thing to money you have collected?
- s7-l2 Which single plank — a person, a platform, a supplier, an API — drops the whole bridge if it snaps? What is your 30-day plan if it snaps tomorrow?
- s7-l3 [names] Who is crossing with you — and what commitment has each actually made, out loud?
- s7-l4 [list] If revenue halves for two quarters, what goes first, second, third? Decide while you are calm.
- s7-l5 [list] What are you deliberately not doing? Strategy is sacrifice — name three.
Milestones: unit walk-through written · SPOF + 30-day plan · three deliberate nots.

**⛩ Act III Gate — The Far Bank** (after Stage 7): unit walk-through answered · SPOF question answered.

## Stage 8 · Launch — *Return with the Elixir* · The Rocket
- s8-th [spine, evidence-locked] The elixir is the story. Tell it with your customer as the hero and you as the guide: 'Once there was [named customer]. Every day, [struggle]. Until one day, [your work]. Because of that, [observed outcome]. Until finally, [transformation].' Every beat cites evidence, or it does not cast. *(uncited spine renders an [unproven] warning)*
- s8-l1 [number] One number tells you it is flying. Which one, why that one, and what is this month's honest target?
- s8-l2 [story] What did this journey disprove that you believed at the start? Write it for the next founder — that is the wisdom you return with.
- s8-l3 **[Joy — survives every rewrite]** How will I celebrate crossing the final threshold?
- s8-l4 Are you ready to let go and let it fly?
Milestones: spine cast from cited evidence · one honest number and target · wisdom for the next founder.

## Family Dinner *(facilitator mode; structurally excluded from all serialization)*
Rules of the table: no bragging · share what's going wrong · everyone talks · if you can help, help. Buckets: **Been there** (pair them) · **Now that's interesting** (rally) · **Do not pass Go** (intractable — route around it). Ninety minutes, tops. What's said at dinner stays at dinner.

## Weather *(one tap, once a day, founder-owned)*

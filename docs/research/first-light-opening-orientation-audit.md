## 2. FINDINGS (sorted by severity)

### BLOCKER

**F1 (#24) — First-kill XP and Truth-denominator both violate canon.** Claim: the opening's "first kill" invalidates "I already know who my customer is," supported only by the founder's own admission (E0/E1), and awards "+15 invalidation XP with real celebration." Canon (02-architecture): XP is paid "per assumption with tier≥2: invalidated +15, validated +10"; and Truth = Σ weight(resolved with tier≥2) / Σ weight over ALL registered assumptions. Two precise effects: (a) an E0/E1-resolved assumption earns **zero** XP under the formula, so the promised +15 is uncanonical; (b) registering the assumption and resolving it without tier≥2 evidence adds its weight to the Truth **denominator** but never the numerator, permanently lowering the founder's Truth ceiling — the tutorial mechanically penalizes completion. Epistemic label: VERIFIED against supplied canon. Verdict: canon-breaking as written. Amendment options (operator must choose, do not auto-pick): **(A)** Stage the first kill as a scripted *lesson* that does NOT write a real Registry entry — cost: collides with the report's own "every beat produces a REAL artifact" defense and risks the human-contact test (a simulated kill). **(B)** Define the founder's own written admission as a distinct evidence class that counts as tier≥2 for self-referential epistemology assumptions only — requires an operator canon ruling and a tier-table amendment. **(C)** Add an explicit XP/Truth carve-out: tutorial-tagged assumptions are excluded from the Truth denominator and pay a fixed "first-light" XP outside the tier≥2 formula. Recommended default to put to the operator: **(C)**, because it preserves a real artifact and real celebration without corrupting the live Truth metric.

### MAJOR

Amendment (paste): "Bind the Chart/Quest Map to **M**; keep Tab = cycle-what-is-near unchanged. Legend on **L**." Any rebind of a shipped control requires an operator ruling AND a same-turn code+docs change (HUD help string + keybind map).

**F3 (#26) — Mentor identity entanglement.** Report names the mentor "the Raven (Corvus / 'the Cartographer's voice')," merging three canon-adjacent entities: the Raven (World-2 *symbol*, not an established character), the Cartographer (one of the Council's three API-gated voices), and a new mentor character. Resolution depends on the Raven canonically being the World-2 mentor (open per the report's own Open Question 3). Operator decision block drafted in §4.

**F4 (#27) — Telemetry conflict.** Report's Stage-1 recommendation: "Instrument it: log completion rate, per-beat drop-off, skip rate, and time-to-first-assumption," with thresholds (≥70% completion, skip <25%, >15% per-beat drop). Canon: no analytics/telemetry backend exists, founder data lives in localStorage only, data minimization is a stated design law. VERIFIED conflict. Amendment options: **(A)** local-only counters surfaced to the founder themselves (zero external signal; no aggregate product learning); **(B)** opt-in anonymous beacon — requires a NEW consent surface + operator ruling + a server endpoint that currently does not exist (breaks "no event-tracking infrastructure"); **(C)** manual playtest observation on the named beta cohort (highest fidelity, lowest scale). Recommended default: **(C)** for the beta, **(A)** thereafter.

**F5 (#28) — s1-l1 double-consumption.** The opening elicits s1-l1 ("[names] Who exactly has this problem? Three real people…") as the first assumption, but s1-l1 is a World-1 shrine question and "three real people named" is a Stage-1 milestone. Failure mode 1: answering in the induction pre-completes the World-1 shrine, breaking World 1's content arc and the milestone economy. Failure mode 2: re-asking in World 1 duplicates the prompt, violating "demanding but kind." VERIFIED conflict. Amendment options: (i) opening consumes s1-l1 and World 1 renders it answered with a revisit/edit affordance; (ii) opening uses a DIFFERENT elicitation that produces an assumption without consuming a canon question; (iii) opening uses the s1-th [story] threshold beat instead. Operator decision block in §4.

**F6 (#34) — New persisted schema is unspecified and uncovered by A-101.** The opening implies new localStorage keys with no canon data-model home. Enumerated schema-delta (ready for canon commit): `openingCompletedAt: string|null`, `openingSkippedAt: string|null`, `invitationSeen: bool`, `chartUnlocked: bool`, `firstLightArtifactIds: string[]`, `openingBeatProgress: {beat:int, ts:string}|null` (for resume-mid-induction), `ravenReentryState: {worldId:int, seen:bool}[]` (only if F11/#33 accepted). Migration rule: default all keys via `{...EMPTY_DATA, ...loaded}`. VERIFIED that the A-101 schema additions cover none of these. Amendment: add the above block to the data model in the same commit as the addendum; without it, resume-mid-induction and skip-state are undefined behavior.

### MINOR

**F7 (#22) — First evidence entry mislabels tier.** The induction logs "I texted Sarah; she said she hates her current spreadsheet" as E2 'Said'. Canon: E2/'Word' is a verbatim quote; a paraphrase is E1 'Heard'. As written this is a paraphrase, so E1 is correct. VERIFIED teaching hazard: the opening would model sloppy tier discipline in the founder's very first evidence entry. Amendment: either render the example as a true verbatim quote ("Sarah, verbatim: 'I hate my spreadsheet, it's the worst part of my week' → E2") or label it E1 and show the founder how to upgrade it to E2 by capturing the exact words.

**F8 (#29) — Vault confiscation beat.** Canon Vault: solution ideas captured with two taps, zero justification, visible, sealed until Stage 3 (`vault:[{id,text,date}], vaultUnlocked:bool`). Report beat: mentor invites naming the solution and sealing it. No mechanical conflict (the Vault is captured-visible-sealed, not secret). VERIFIED compatible. Amendment: confirm copy avoids any "confiscation"/shaming framing (voice laws: never shame); frame as "we'll keep this safe and come back to it," not "you're not allowed this yet."

**F9 (#30) — Trough drawn W4–W6.** Report asserts the emotional dip spans W4–W6 and the map road should dip there. Canon references a "mid-journey winter" (PIE rollercoaster lineage) without pinning stages. Label W4–W6 as the report's INFERENCE, not canon. Drawing it on the map makes it quasi-canonical; requires operator sign-off before it becomes load-bearing chrome.

**F10 (#31) — Chart-as-Cartographer's-artifact.** Elegant but binds a free, offline UI surface to an API-gated paid entity (same blur as F3). One consent-copy sentence resolves it. Amendment (paste): "The Chart is yours — it works offline and costs nothing. The Cartographer only speaks when you convene the Council."

**F11 (#33) — Raven at every shrine.** Net-new persistent entity across all 8 worlds with per-world art/interaction cost and the `ravenReentryState` schema (F6). Cheaper v1: help-menu replay of the induction + a one-time World-1 re-entry prompt. Recommend shipping the cheap version first; defer the per-shrine perch to a later phase.

**F12 (#37/#39) — Cinematic and length risks.** DOF post-processing (`@react-three/postprocessing` Bokeh) is the heaviest item in the report's cinematic vocabulary and is heavier than implied for a solo builder on low-end hardware; letterboxing (CSS DOM), camera dollies (drei), and typewriter-with-instant-complete (DOM) are all lightweight. The 11 beats sum to roughly 10.5–12 min at the high end; typing beats (naming three people, writing a kill criterion, logging evidence) plausibly take 2–4× the budgeted time for slow typists, putting the 15-minute hard ceiling at risk. Amendment: gate DOF behind `prefers-reduced-motion`/quality toggle; re-budget typing beats at 2–3× and either trim a beat or raise the ceiling.

### NIT

**F13 (#21) — FTUE timing self-contradiction.** Section B target: first "win" within 60–90s. The 11-beat sequence places the first assembled assumption at beat 6. Summing the report's own beat durations (beat1 ~20s + beat2 ~30–45s + beat3 ~90s + beat4 ~90s + beat5 ~120s) puts beat 6 at **~5.8–6.1 minutes**, not 60–90s. VERIFIED contradiction. Recommended resolution: redefine the 60–90s target as "first meaningful input" (movement at beat 3) and reserve "first assumption captured" for its real ~6-min slot, OR restructure beats to pull a lightweight capture earlier.

**F14 (#23) — Quiet skip spec.** Matches the operator ruling (required first time, small non-descript off-to-the-side button, invitation with value prop). VERIFIED, no drift.

**F15 (#32) — Skip ≠ gate override.** Canon overrides require a written reason + logging; the operator ruled the opening skip is a small button with NO written reason. Confirm the report does not subject the skip to gate-override mechanics (it does not), and state the exemption explicitly in the addendum so canon stays coherent: "The opening skip is exempt from override-logging; it is a courtesy control, not a gate."

**F16 (#35) — Accessibility feasibility.** No-timed-reading, reduced-motion, resizable text, ARIA on DOM dialogue, and audio sliders are all feasible in the R3F+DOM architecture. Build-prompt language required: a `prefers-reduced-motion` media query must gate camera dollies and letterbox animation; DOM dialogue nodes need ARIA live-region roles.

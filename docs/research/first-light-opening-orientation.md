# Founder's Quest: Opening Sequence & Orientation System — Research & Design

## TL;DR
- **Build a bounded, mentor-led "First Light" induction modeled on Breath of the Wild's Great Plateau and Outer Wilds' Observatory: a small walled garden (~8–12 minutes) where the founder learns the epistemology AND controls by DOING real validation work — eliciting their real first assumption, sealing a real kill criterion, and logging their first real evidence — before the fence drops and World 1 opens.** The cold-drop must be replaced, but the fix is not a cutscene; it is a playable microcosm of the whole game.
- **Mentor recommendation: a NEW named mentor — the Raven (Corvus / "the Cartographer's voice") — delivered as pre-written text-and-camera dialogue, NOT live AI.** This resolves the Council's cost/consent problem (no API spend at first run), honors "the app is the mentor" (the Raven is the app's diegetic face, not a separate character with its own agenda), and resolves the Stage-2 collision by making First Light a *prologue visitation* — the Raven greets you at the threshold and then flies ahead to World 2, where the full "Meeting the Mentor" beat pays off.
- **The legend and Quest Map should be one diegetic artifact — the Cartographer's Chart — recallable on demand (Tab = map, L = legend), rendered in cel-shaded parchment.** First-run teaches it inline; forever after it is a pull-out reference. The invitation is a required-but-escapable door with a warm, plain value-prop and a small, findable (never hidden) skip; skippers are offered the Raven's return at any shrine.

## Key Findings

1. **The gold standard is not a tutorial that precedes the game — it is the game in miniature.** BOTW's Great Plateau confines the player behind a literal cliff-fence until four shrines teach the four core systems, then hands over the paraglider as the threshold key. Polygon's Ryan Gilliam ("The 13 best video game intros," Feb 20, 2022) declared it the "best tutorial ever made," describing it as a miniature version of Breath of the Wild; ScreenRant's Kyle Gratton separately called it "the Gold Standard of Zelda Tutorials" (Mar 24, 2021). Its genius is that it is a microcosm: freedom within a fence, all systems taught by doing, a diegetic mentor (the Old Man) who is optional. FQ should compress this to minutes but copy the *shape*.

2. **Front-loaded worldview induction works ONLY when it is explorable and diegetic, not narrated.** Outer Wilds teaches its entire epistemology — the time loop, the astronomy, the translation tool — through an interactive museum/observatory the player walks through before the first launch; the observatory is the only mandatory tutorial element (you must visit to get launch codes). This is the single most relevant prior art for FQ because FQ's "worldview" (evidence tiers, Truth vs Action, falsification-as-progress) is genuinely novel and must be taught, not assumed.

3. **Mario World 1-1 and kishōtenketsu prove you can teach without text — but FQ's concepts are abstract, so it needs a hybrid.** Miyamoto's four-beat kishōtenketsu structure (introduce → develop → twist → test) teaches every mechanic without text. Movement and shrine-interaction can be taught Mario-style (architecture, affordances, no text). But E0–E4 tiers, the Truth/Action distinction, and the Vault are conceptual, not spatial — they need a mentor's plain words plus a live demonstration on the HUD.

4. **First-time-user experience is where games die.** Per GameAnalytics' Q1 2024 benchmark study (10,000+ projects, 2.7B MAU), median D1 retention across all projects is 22.91% (D7 4.2%, D28 0.85%); top-quartile D1 is 26.48–27.69%, splitting to 31–33% on iOS vs 25–27% on Android; AppsFlyer's mobile-games figure is ~29.46%. Poor FTUE spikes day-1 churn. For FQ, "churn" is a founder abandoning the disciplined validation practice — the exact failure the game exists to prevent — so the stakes of the opening are higher than for entertainment games.

5. **Mandatory tutorials breed resentment when they are unskippable, repeated, and slow — but "gradual engagement" (Duolingo) defuses it.** Duolingo defers signup until after the first lesson; "Moving the sign-up screen behind the first lesson increased DAUs by 20%," per Duolingo's growth team via First Round Review ("People thought we were crazy — why would you let users in without capturing their email? But the data was unambiguous."). The lesson: let the player DO the real thing first, then formalize. FQ's "required with quiet skip" is the correct instinct; the key is that the induction must be real work, not a gate before real work.

6. **Diegetic reference systems (Tunic's manual, Hades' Codex, Dark Souls item text, Outer Wilds' ship log) outperform pause-menu glossaries because they carry worldbuilding and are pleasurable to consult.** Per GamesRadar+ ("Tunic brings back game manuals with a clever play on Zelda 2 nostalgia"), developers are "calling Tunic's manual a brilliant and economical narrative tool that blends tutorials with graphic design to great effect" (a 56-page in-game manual inspired by the Zelda II NES booklet). CK3's nested tooltips-within-tooltips + Encyclopedia is the reference model for explaining a complex-stat HUD without walls of text.

7. **Anti-punitive gamification is empirically grounded.** Diefenbach & Müssig (2019, IJHCS Vol. 127: pp. 190–210, July 2019; "Counterproductive effects of gamification: An analysis on the example of the gamified task manager Habitica"; Study 2 = quantitative field study, 45 users over two weeks) found, verbatim from the abstract: "All participants experienced counterproductive effects to some degree, whereby some effects (e.g., being punished by Habitica in especially productive times, since one does not manage to check off tasks in time) were more prevalent than others." Counterproductive effects were correlated to perceived inappropriateness of the reward system and predictive of motivation decline. This directly validates FQ's no-punitive-streaks canon and means the opening must never threaten, shame, or impose loss.

## Details

### A. Prior-Art Survey (named games → extractable lesson → FQ application)

**Breath of the Wild — The Great Plateau (the master template).** Bounded space, four shrines each teaching one rune ability, weather/temperature/stamina taught by terrain, the Old Man as optional diegetic mentor, and the paraglider as the literal threshold-crossing artifact. The player can ignore the Old Man entirely — freedom within a fence. Nintendo's PR (Reggie Fils-Aimé) noted the Plateau is ~2% of the game map yet held two full days of E3 2016 Treehouse content — a testament to density-within-bounds. *Borrow:* the walled-garden structure, one-concept-per-station, the mentor-you-can-ignore, the earned threshold object. *Avoid:* the 1–2 hour length; FQ compresses to ~10 minutes. *FQ application:* "First Light" is a small nebula-edge clearing with 3–4 rune shrines, each teaching one pillar (evidence tiers, Truth vs Action, the Vault, falsification). The threshold artifact is the Cartographer's Chart (map+legend), handed over when the founder has logged their first real evidence.

**Outer Wilds — the Observatory (worldview-as-explorable-exhibit).** The museum teaches the game's entire epistemology diegetically before the first loop; the only *mandatory* element is visiting to get launch codes. *Borrow:* teaching a novel worldview through walk-up interactive exhibits rather than a lecture. *FQ application:* each shrine is an "exhibit" the founder activates to see a mechanic demonstrated live (e.g., the Truth exhibit shows a sample assumption moving from E1→E3 and the Truth bar staying flat until E2+).

**Super Mario Bros. World 1-1 + kishōtenketsu (teach by architecture).** Miyamoto's four-beat structure teaches every mechanic without text. *Borrow:* teach WASD/arrows and shrine-approach purely through the clearing's layout — the first shrine is unmissable, dead ahead. *Avoid:* relying on architecture for abstract concepts. *FQ application:* mechanics (movement, interact, trance-input) are taught Mario-style; epistemology is taught by the Raven's words + live HUD demonstration.

**The Matrix — the Construct/dojo (worldview reveal → first practice → "show me").** Morpheus loads the rules ("some can be bent, others broken"), then immediately spars: "Stop trying to hit me and hit me." Knowledge, then embodied practice. *Borrow:* the beat where the mentor states a rule and then *makes you do it* in a safe sparring space. *FQ application:* the Raven explains "only Seen and Paid move Truth," then immediately: "Now — tell me one thing you believe about your customer that you have only heard, never seen." The founder types; the machine logs it as E1; the Truth bar does not move. Lesson embodied. (Also note Morpheus's "I can only show you the door; you're the one that has to walk through it" — the exact register for a mentor who must never simulate contact.)

**Star Wars — Obi-Wan's lightsaber handoff (artifact → worldview → threshold).** The canonical mentor beat: artifact handoff, worldview reveal, first practice, threshold crossing. Note Obi-Wan is *supernatural aid* met at the threshold; the *deeper* mentor training (Yoda) comes later. *FQ application (the inversion):* FQ inverts the artifact handoff. Obi-Wan GIVES Luke the lightsaber; the Raven TAKES the founder's "lightsaber" — their solution idea — and seals it in the Vault. This is the single most resonant beat available to FQ: the founder arrives clutching their solution; the mentor gently confiscates it ("This is your blade. It is real, and it is yours. But a blade drawn too early cuts the wielder. I am sealing it until you have earned the right to swing it — at Stage 3."). This teaches the Vault, Act I discipline, and "the customer is the hero" in one gesture.

**Hades — Achilles/Nyx (mentor woven into a loop, failure as progress).** Achilles authors the Codex and encourages Zagreus through repeated death; the game makes you "look forward to death." *Borrow:* the mentor who reframes failure as progress — directly parallel to FQ's "invalidation pays 1.5× XP." *FQ application:* the Raven frames the first kill as a win, with real celebration, so the founder learns viscerally that killing an assumption makes the map truer.

**Bastion — reactive narrator (orientation as ambient commentary).** The narrator reacts to player actions in short, terse lines; Supergiant's stated rules were "keep it short" and no repeats. *Borrow:* the Raven can react to founder actions with short lines ("Heard, not seen. The bar holds."). *Avoid:* full reactive narration is a huge content-authoring load for a solo builder; use sparingly, scripted to specific trigger points, not generatively.

**Tunic — collectible instruction manual (the manual IS the artifact).** *Borrow:* the legend/map as a beautiful, ownable diegetic object (the Cartographer's Chart) rather than a sterile menu. *Avoid:* Tunic gates comprehension behind scattered collectibles — wrong for FQ, where clarity is an ethic. FQ hands over the full Chart at threshold; it fills in as worlds are walked.

**CK3 — nested tooltips + Encyclopedia (explaining a complex stat HUD).** Tooltips-within-tooltips let players pile up explanations; "Noob Island" (Ireland) is a bounded diegetic tutorial; a reactive-advice widget suggests next actions. *Borrow:* every HUD element (Truth, Action, tier chips, Shadow, Weather Trail, Vault) gets a hover/focus tooltip whose highlighted terms open deeper tooltips. This is the recall mechanism after first-run. *FQ application:* legend terms like "E2+" are clickable to reveal "why Hunches never move Truth."

**Undertale — Toriel (the mentor who walks you through, and what it subverts).** Toriel over-protects, solving puzzles for you; the game later subverts this. *Borrow:* awareness that a mentor who does the work FOR the founder violates FQ's human-contact test. The Raven must never simulate contact or answer for the customer — it points at the door, it does not walk through it.

**Journey / Hollow Knight (wordless vs minimal).** Journey inducts wordlessly; Hollow Knight's minimalism has real discoverability costs. *Lesson:* FQ cannot be wordless (concepts too abstract) and cannot be minimal-cryptic (founders need clarity fast). FQ sits in the middle: plain words, load-bearing joy, no mystery about the mechanics.

**Portal — GLaDOS (antagonist-as-tutorial-voice).** The test chambers teach through an untrustworthy guide. *Lesson for FQ:* the Shadow could someday narrate a hard lesson, but the *induction* mentor must be trustworthy — a founder disoriented on first run needs a guide they can trust, not an ironic one. Reserve the antagonist voice for later.

### B. Onboarding & Tutorial Theory

**The tutorialization spectrum** runs from front-loaded (all teaching before play) to just-in-time/contextual (teach at point of need). The literature and the prior art both favor contextual, but FQ's novel epistemology forces a *bounded front-load*: you cannot let a founder log E1 evidence without first explaining what E1 means. The resolution is the Great Plateau model — a front-loaded space that is itself playable, so teaching and doing are the same act. This is also the answer to the **chocolate-covered-broccoli** failure mode (a term coined by Brenda Laurel in *Utopian Entrepreneur*, 2001; the serious-games literature — e.g., Farber, Edutopia — warns that sugar-coating boring content fools no one): the induction must not *delay* real work with a fun wrapper — the induction must BE the first real validation work. The founder's actual venture is the material from minute one.

**Teach one thing at a time / spacing.** Each shrine teaches exactly one pillar; the founder practices it before the next appears. This maps to progressive disclosure (Miller's Law: working memory holds ~5–9 items; reveal complexity gradually) and to kishōtenketsu's one-mechanic-per-level discipline.

**Flow and player-type tolerance.** Csikszentmihalyi's flow requires challenge matched to skill; a first session must avoid both boredom (over-explaining) and anxiety (overload). Because FQ founders are adults doing real work (not entertainment-seekers), tutorial tolerance is *higher if the tutorial is visibly real work* and *lower if it feels like a game-y detour*. This argues for making the value-prop explicit ("this is your first real validation, not a warm-up").

**FTUE / retention data.** As above, median mobile D1 retention is ~23% (GameAnalytics Q1 2024), with poor FTUE spiking day-1 churn. Onboarding drop-off is steep: analytics vendor devtodev reports, verbatim, "in general, only 40% of the players pass the tutorial! Admit that it is not enough. The optimal value is 70–80%"; DeltaDNA (via Udonis) reports nearly 20% of players don't complete even the first tutorial quest. Industry guidance targets first meaningful action/first reward within ~30–60 seconds. *FQ implication:* the first genuine "win" (first assumption captured, bar animating) must land within the first ~60–90 seconds of the induction, not at its end.

**Mandatory-tutorial resentment.** Unskippable, repeated, slow tutorials (Mega Man X5's Alia, Assassin's Creed's first hour, Ocarina's Navi) generate documented backlash; a fan patch removing Alia was downloaded over three thousand times. The defusing patterns: (a) let the player act fast, (b) never repeat on replay, (c) allow escape. *FQ implication:* the "required with quiet skip" model is sound; the induction must be genuinely escapable and must never re-trigger on subsequent visits.

**Accessibility (non-negotiable for a text-heavy induction).** Per the Game Accessibility Guidelines and Xbox Accessibility Guidelines: (1) never timed reading — dialogue advances only on player input (typewriter reveal with instant-complete on keypress, never auto-advance); (2) reduced-motion toggle for camera dollies, letterbox transitions, and the painted-sky parallax (Xbox XAG-117: let players pause/stop content that scrolls, blinks, or auto-updates, and adjust camera/FOV); (3) high-contrast, resizable text (≥16pt base, scalable to 150–200%); (4) screen-reader-friendly semantic structure for all dialogue and legend text (this is a React DOM app, so ARIA labeling is feasible and should be specified); (5) independent audio sliders; (6) a persistent, plain-language restatement of the current objective. Every one of these is an FTUE benefit for all players, not just disabled ones.

### C. The Legend / HUD-Key System — "The Cartographer's Chart" (recto: Legend)

**Recommendation: the legend and the Quest Map are two faces of one diegetic artifact, the Cartographer's Chart, owned by the founder and handed over at the induction's threshold beat.** This ties the orientation system to existing canon: the Council's third voice is the Cartographer, so the Chart is diegetically the Cartographer's gift — the map/legend system is already grounded in canon rather than bolted on.

**First-run legend walkthrough (inline, during induction).** As each HUD element first becomes relevant, it animates in and the Raven names it in one plain clause (progressive disclosure — "HUD elements unlock as systems unlock"). Order of introduction:
1. **The five tier chips (E0–E4 / Whisper·Rumor·Word·Deed·Gold).** Introduced when the founder logs their first evidence. The chip lights at the tier logged. Plain gloss: "Whisper: a hunch. Rumor: you heard it. Word: they said it to you. Deed: you saw them do it. Gold: they paid."
2. **The Truth bar.** Introduced immediately after: "Only Deed and Gold — Seen and Paid — move this bar. Hunches never move it, no matter who whispers them." Critically, the legend must explain the counterintuitive state where Truth sits at 0% while evidence banks: the canon line "evidence banked, the verdict comes at the Mirror" (World 5) should be the tooltip text, so a founder is never alarmed by a flat bar.
3. **The Action bar.** "This tracks what you've done — milestones, self-reported. It moves easily. That's the danger."
4. **The Shadow indicator.** "When you do much but know little — high Action, low Truth — a rival stirs. Not to punish you. To remind you." (Anti-punitive framing per Diefenbach & Müssig.) The legend notes the cadence-law exception: the Shadow is suppressed during the emotional trough (Weather Trail last-3 mean ≤2).
5. **The Weather Trail.** "Your mood, logged. The road dips in the middle for everyone. When it dips, the game eases off."
6. **The Vault icon (sealed/unsealed).** Introduced at the solution-confiscation beat: "Sealed until Stage 3. Your ideas are safe. They are not lost."
7. **XP.** "You earn more for killing a belief than confirming one. Fifteen for a kill, ten for a confirm. Wrong maps get you lost."

**Persistent recall affordance.** Keybind **L** (and a small chart-corner icon in the HUD) opens the Legend face at any time, game paused. Every term uses **CK3-style nested tooltips**: hover/focus "E2+" → tooltip → highlighted "Truth" inside it → deeper tooltip. This is the mechanism that lets the first-run stay short (teach the minimum) while making everything recoverable on demand (depth available forever). The legend lives as a **diegetic overlay** (the Chart unfurls over a dimmed game), not a sterile pause menu and not a separate screen — this preserves the mythic chrome while keeping the ask (the definitions) plain.

### D. The Quest Map — "The Cartographer's Chart" (verso: Map)

**Recommendation: a pull-out parchment map (keybind Tab, plus HUD icon) showing all 8 worlds as waypoints along a single winding pilgrimage road, rendered in cel-shaded low-poly parchment consistent with the Phase-2b art direction.** Contents and rules:

- **All 8 worlds shown with mythic + stage names:** W1 Swirling Nebula (The Problem / Call to Adventure), W2 The Raven (Research / Meeting the Mentor), W3 The Phoenix (Prototyping / The Approach), W4 The Labyrinth (Testing / Crossing the Threshold), W5 The Mirror (Feedback / Tests, Allies & Enemies), W6 The Sculptor (Refinement / The Ordeal), W7 The Bridge (Implementation / The Road Back), W8 The Rocket (Launch / Return with the Elixir).
- **Current position** marked by a glowing rune-token (the founder's marker).
- **Act gates after W2 and after W5** shown as archways/waystations on the road — thresholds, not locked doors.
- **Open vs ahead:** per "gates warn, never block," future worlds render as *not-yet-walked* (softer, mist-veiled, path drawn but faint) — never as locked-forbidden with a padlock. The road is visible the whole way; you simply haven't walked it yet. This mirrors BOTW showing Hyrule Castle in the distance from the Plateau.
- **The rollercoaster emotional arc (the PIE map lineage):** the road should visibly *dip* through W4–W6 (the Labyrinth/Mirror/Sculptor trough) and rise toward W8. Foreshadowing the trough on the map normalizes it per the cadence law — a founder who sees "the road dips here for everyone" is inoculated against interpreting their own mid-journey despair as failure. This is a load-bearing use of the map, not decoration.
- **Per-world state:** each waypoint shows a compact Truth/Action pip pair (two tiny bars) so the founder can read progress at a glance, plus **killed-assumption tombstones** accumulating at each world (persistent memorials, per the antagonist-design research) — a visible graveyard of slain beliefs that grows as the map gets truer. This makes invalidation feel like accumulation, not loss.
- **Diegetic framing:** the map is the Cartographer's artifact. When opened, a faint Cartographer voice-line (pre-written, not live-AI) may greet the founder on first open ("Every road looks long from the start. Walk it a waypoint at a time.").

### E. The Invitation & The Quiet Skip

**The invitation screen (first thing the founder sees).** Full-bleed painted nebula, the Raven alighting, one card of copy. The value-prop must be plain, warm, second-person, no marketing language, no hedging (canon voice laws). Recommended copy approach:

> **Before you begin.**
> You could drop straight into the work. Most founders who do get lost — not because the work is hard, but because no one showed them the rules of this place.
> Let me show you. It takes about ten minutes. You'll do real work — your first real assumption, your first real evidence — not a rehearsal. Everything I show you, you can call back any time with the map and the legend.
> **[ Walk with me ]** (primary)
> *skip for now* (secondary, small, lower-right, muted — never hidden)

Design principles behind the copy: leads with the founder's own risk (disorientation), states the exact time cost, promises real work (not a warm-up — kills the chocolate-broccoli suspicion), and pre-answers the "will I lose this?" fear (everything is recallable). No exclamation marks, no "amazing," no flattery. This mirrors Duolingo's low-anchor time framing ("2 minutes") and gradual-engagement principle without copying its upsell tactics.

**The skip affordance — the honesty line between "quiet" and "deceptive."** FQ's ethos forbids dark patterns. The skip must be *findable, not hidden*: (a) present on the same screen, not behind a menu; (b) lower-right, small, muted-but-legible (meets contrast minimums — de-emphasized ≠ invisible); (c) labeled plainly ("skip for now"), never guilt-tripped ("No thanks, I like being lost"). The de-emphasis is legitimate visual hierarchy (secondary action styled as secondary); crossing into deception would be hiding it, shrinking it below legibility, or shaming the choice. FQ stays on the honest side.

**What a skipper misses, and re-entry.** A skipper drops into World 1 without the guided first assumption/first evidence/first kill and without the inline legend walkthrough — but they still receive the Cartographer's Chart (map+legend) in their HUD, so nothing is permanently gated (gates warn, never block). Re-entry affordances: (1) the Raven perches at every shrine and can be re-approached to run "First Light" at any time ("a mentor you can revisit"); (2) a "Return to First Light" entry in the help/pause menu; (3) on first World-1 shrine interaction, a one-time gentle prompt: "You skipped the orientation. Want it now, or carry on?" — offered once, never nagged (defusing resentment per the forced-tutorial literature). On replay/subsequent app visits, the opening is skippable normally (never re-triggers as mandatory).

### F. The First Assumption, First Evidence, First Kill — Induction as Real Work

**This is where FQ either honors or violates its soul.** The human-contact test forbids simulation; the induction must produce REAL artifacts from the founder's actual venture.

**First assumption (real).** The Raven asks a canon Stage-1 question whose answer becomes the founder's first registered assumption with a real, sealed kill criterion. Recommendation: use **s1-l1 "Who exactly has this problem? Name three real people."** It is concrete (typed-answer type: list of names), it is genuinely theirs, and it produces an immediately falsifiable claim ("these three people have this problem"). The Raven then guides an **Ariadne's Thread** beat: "Before you look, decide what would prove you wrong. If you talk to all three and none has tried to solve this in the last month — is the assumption dead?" The founder types an IF-THEN kill criterion; it is sealed and timestamped BEFORE any evidence exists (canon). This teaches falsification-as-progress with the founder's own material — and it operationalizes the Mom Test (past behavior over future opinion: "what have they actually done about this," never "would you use my thing").

**First kill — resolve the honesty tension.** A *simulated dummy assumption* that gets invalidated would violate the human-contact test and teach the founder that the machine fakes outcomes. **Recommendation: the scripted "first kill" targets a REAL epistemology-level assumption every founder actually holds — "I already know who my customer is."** This is honest: it is a belief the founder genuinely holds, it can be safely invalidated in-session by the founder's own admission (the Raven asks "Have you watched three of them try to solve this with their own hands and money? …Then it is Heard, not Seen"), and the invalidation is REAL — the founder really does hold a weaker evidentiary position than they felt. The Raven logs "I know my customer" as E0/E1, shows it cannot move Truth, and ceremonially "kills" the inflated confidence — awarding the +15 invalidation XP and a genuine celebration. Nothing is faked; the founder's real epistemic state is surfaced and corrected. This is the Matrix "how did I beat you?" beat: the lesson lands because it happened to them, not to a dummy.

**The Vault introduction (the inverted lightsaber handoff).** The founder arrives holding a solution idea. Early in First Light, the Raven invites them to name it ("What did you come here to build?"), captures it verbatim to the Vault, and seals it — the confiscation beat described in Section A. Solution-language detection (canon) nudges further capture if the founder keeps describing features. This teaches the Vault, Act I discipline, and "the app is the mentor" (the Raven acts in the founder's long-term interest against their short-term impulse).

**First evidence logged live (the machine works before their eyes).** The founder logs one real piece of evidence about one of their three named people (e.g., "I texted Sarah; she said she hates her current spreadsheet" = E2 Said → Word). The tier chip lights, XP ticks, and — crucially — the Truth bar animates or *pointedly does not* (if the entry is E1). Seeing the machine respond to a real input is the "I know kung fu / show me" payoff: knowledge becomes embodied.

**Recommended full sequence (validated against prior art), ~8–12 minutes:**
1. **Invitation** (~20s) — the door with the quiet skip. *Teaches:* consent, value-prop, time cost.
2. **Mythic cold open** (~30–45s) — camera dolly across the Swirling Nebula, painted sky, the Raven descends; letterboxed, reduced-motion-respecting. *Teaches:* tone, register, "you are the hero of a quest." (Journey/BOTW awakening.)
3. **Mentor meet + controls** (~90s) — the Raven greets; founder learns WASD/arrows and shrine-approach by walking to the first shrine (Mario 1-1 architecture). *Teaches:* movement, interaction, trance-input.
4. **Worldview beat 1 — the Vault confiscation** (~90s) — name your solution; the Raven seals it. *Teaches:* the Vault, Act I discipline, the inverted-artifact handoff.
5. **Worldview beat 2 — tiers + Truth vs Action** (~2min) — the Truth exhibit shrine; the Raven states the rule and makes the founder classify a real belief (Matrix "show me"). *Teaches:* E0–E4, Truth moves only on E2+, Action moves easily.
6. **First assumption registered** (~90s) — s1-l1 "three real people"; sealed kill criterion (Ariadne's Thread). *Teaches:* falsification, typed answers, kill criteria before results.
7. **First evidence logged live** (~60s) — one real datapoint; HUD animates. *Teaches:* the ledger works; banked ≠ verdict.
8. **First kill** (~90s) — the real epistemology-level assumption invalidated; +15 XP; celebration. *Teaches:* invalidation pays more; killing makes the map truer; joy is load-bearing.
9. **Legend walkthrough** (~60s, mostly already introduced inline) — the Raven hands over the Chart; quick tour of L. *Teaches:* recall affordance.
10. **Quest Map handoff** (~45s) — the Chart's map face; the Raven shows the road, the trough, the eight worlds, then says it will fly ahead to World 2. *Teaches:* the journey shape; normalizes the trough; sets up the Stage-2 mentor payoff.
11. **Threshold crossing** (~30s) — the fence (nebula edge) parts; World 1 proper opens. *Teaches:* agency begins. (BOTW paraglider beat.)

This validates the operator's proposed sequence with two refinements: the Vault confiscation moves EARLIER (beat 4, as the emotional hook) and the first evidence is logged BEFORE the first kill (so the founder sees the machine work on neutral input before the emotionally charged invalidation).

### G. Production Feasibility for a Solo Builder (R3F stack)

**Cinematic vocabulary without voice acting or bespoke animation:**
- **Camera as the primary cinematic tool.** R3F camera dollies, slow pushes, and rack-focus (via post-processing DOF) carry the mythic weight. Letterbox bars (two CSS/DOM divs or a fullscreen quad) instantly signal "cinematic." Lighting shifts (warm keylight per the art-direction addendum, a rune-shrine glow ramp) do emotional work cheaply. All are trivial in R3F.
- **Text presentation.** Letterboxed dialogue with typewriter reveal, instant-complete on keypress, NEVER timed (accessibility + canon). This is a small React component over a JSON script — the cheapest possible "acting."
- **The Raven model.** A CC0 low-poly raven/bird from Quaternius or Kenney, cel-shaded to match. Idle + a single "wing-flare" gesture is enough; the Raven can perch (static) most of the time and use camera cuts to imply presence. No rig-heavy character animation required. If even that is too much for v1, the Raven can be a stylized 2D sigil/silhouette that never needs 3D animation.
- **Audio.** CC0 ambient pads (Kenney/OpenGameArt) + simple UI cues (shrine chime, tier-chip ding, celebration sting). No voice acting. Generated/royalty-free ambient can carry atmosphere; it is a v3 polish item, not a v1 blocker.

**No live AI in the opening (firm).** The Council is BYOK/API-gated and costs the player money; the opening must run entirely on pre-written content with the Raven as scripted text. This also means the opening works offline and on first run before any API key exists — essential, since the invitation is the very first screen. If the operator later wants a live-AI flourish (e.g., the Cartographer reacting to the founder's specific assumption), it must be optional, consented, cost-disclosed, and have a pre-written fallback — but it should NOT be in the minimum lovable opening.

**Where it slots in the 8-phase build.** The game is mid-Phase-2b (World-Feel pass). Recommendation: **build the opening AFTER Phase 2b establishes the visual floor but integrate it as the front of Phase 3 (world-building), because the opening reuses World-1 assets (the Swirling Nebula, shrines, HUD) and must assume the cel-shaded floor, not the grey-box.** Building it before 2b risks authoring against primitives that get thrown away; building it much later risks the cold-drop bad-first-impression persisting through playtests. Front of Phase 3 is the sweet spot.

**Minimum lovable opening vs full vision (phased):**
- **v1 (minimum lovable): text-and-camera induction.** The full 11-beat sequence delivered with camera dollies, letterbox, typewriter text, the Raven as a static perched sigil/silhouette, lighting shifts, and UI cues. No character animation, no voice, no live AI. This is fully buildable by one operator on R3F and delivers the entire worldview induction + first-assumption/evidence/kill + legend + map. **This alone fixes the cold-drop problem.**
- **v2: add the 3D Raven model + a handful of gesture animations + the animated Chart unfurl.**
- **v3: add ambient audio beds, celebration stings, richer shrine VFX, and (optionally, consented) a live-AI Cartographer reaction with pre-written fallback.**

## Recommendations (staged, with thresholds)

**Stage 0 — Lock the design decisions (before any build).**
1. **Adopt the Raven as the named mentor, pre-written text delivery.** Threshold to reconsider: if playtesters report the Raven feels like a *separate character competing with the app's voice* rather than the app's face, collapse it toward an unnamed Cartographer-voice narrator (Bastion-style). Do NOT make the mentor live-AI in the opening under any condition.
2. **Adopt the Cartographer's Chart as the unified legend+map artifact** (L = legend, Tab = map), diegetically the Cartographer's gift.
3. **Adopt the inverted-lightsaber Vault confiscation** as beat 4 and the **real-epistemology-level first kill** (not a simulated dummy).

**Stage 1 — Build v1 (front of Phase 3).** Ship the 11-beat text-and-camera induction reusing World-1/2b assets. Instrument it: log completion rate, per-beat drop-off, skip rate, and time-to-first-assumption. Success thresholds borrowed from industry: **tutorial completion ≥70%** (40% is a redesign trigger, per devtodev), **first meaningful action (first assumption captured) within ~90 seconds**, **skip rate <25%** on first run. If skip rate is high, the invitation copy or length is wrong — cut beats, never hide the skip.

**Stage 2 — Playtest against the failure modes.** Watch real founders. If they describe the induction as "a tutorial I had to get through" rather than "the first real work," it has slid into chocolate-covered broccoli — cut narration, increase real-work density. If they abandon mid-induction, it is too long — the trigger is any beat where >15% drop.

**Stage 3 — Add v2/v3 polish only after v1 clears thresholds.** The Raven model, animations, and audio are additive; do not let them block the cold-drop fix.

**Stage 4 — Accessibility gate before ship.** No timed reading, reduced-motion toggle, resizable/high-contrast text, ARIA labeling on all DOM dialogue/legend, independent audio sliders, persistent objective restatement. This is a hard gate, not a nice-to-have.

## Open Design Questions (each with a recommended default)

1. **Is the mentor the Raven or an unnamed Cartographer-voice?** *Default: the Raven, named, as the app's diegetic face.* Revisit only if playtest signals character-competition.
2. **Does the first kill target "I know my customer" or "my idea is validated"?** *Default: "I already know who my customer is,"* because it flows directly from the s1-l1 "three real people" assumption. Operator ruling needed on exact wording (must pass the never-shame test).
3. **Does the Raven greet the founder again in World 2, or is First Light its only appearance until then?** *Default: the Raven flies ahead at beat 10 and the FULL "Meeting the Mentor" beat pays off in World 2* — this is how the Stage-2 collision resolves. Operator should confirm the Raven is canonically the World-2 mentor figure.
4. **Does the skipper get the one-time "want it now?" prompt on first World-1 shrine, or nothing?** *Default: offer once, never nag.*
5. **How much of the legend is taught inline vs deferred to the L-key?** *Default: teach the 7 elements inline as they arise; defer all depth (nested tooltips) to L.*
6. **Map: single winding road, or node-graph (SMB3-style)?** *Default: single winding pilgrimage road with a visible mid-journey dip,* because the trough-foreshadowing is load-bearing and a node-graph doesn't convey emotional arc.
7. **Exact induction length target?** *Default: 8–12 minutes; hard ceiling 15.* Beyond 15 minutes the resentment risk outweighs the orientation benefit.

## Caveats & Risks

- **Onboarding length is the primary risk.** Too long before agency and the founder resents it (documented backlash against hour-long forced tutorials); too short and the novel epistemology doesn't land. The 8–12 minute target with first-agency at ~90 seconds is a bet, not a certainty — it MUST be validated with per-beat drop-off instrumentation. If founders drop, cut beats before cutting clarity.
- **Mandatory-with-quiet-skip resentment.** The model is sound but fragile. The skip must be genuinely findable and the induction must never re-trigger as mandatory. A single instance of the game re-forcing the opening will poison trust — and FQ's whole thesis is trust.
- **Chocolate-covered broccoli is the subtlest risk.** If the induction feels like a game-y wrapper around a lecture, it fails on FQ's own terms. The defense is that every beat produces a REAL artifact (real assumption, real kill criterion, real evidence). Guard this ferociously in playtests: the moment a beat becomes "pretend," it violates the human-contact test.
- **The first-kill honesty knife-edge.** Invalidating a real belief the founder holds is powerful but must never tip into shaming ("you were wrong to be confident"). The framing must be "you now know something truer" — celebration, +15 XP, warmth. Diefenbach & Müssig's finding that inappropriate/punitive reward systems predict motivation decline is the empirical warning here.
- **Solo-builder scope.** The v1/v2/v3 phasing is the mitigation. The temptation will be to build the Raven model and audio first because they are visible and fun; the discipline is to ship the text-and-camera v1 that actually fixes the cold-drop, then polish. The full vision is not required to solve the stated problem; v1 is.
- **Data-quality caveat on the metrics cited.** The FTUE/retention and tutorial-completion figures (D1 median ~23% per GameAnalytics Q1 2024; completion ~70–80% per devtodev; first-action <60s) come predominantly from industry analytics vendors (GameAnalytics, devtodev, DeltaDNA, AppsFlyer, Chameleon), not peer-reviewed research, and are drawn from entertainment/mobile contexts that differ from FQ's serious-tool context. Treat them as directional benchmarks for instrumentation targets, not laws. The Diefenbach & Müssig (2019) anti-punitive finding is the one peer-reviewed anchor and should carry the most weight.
